6. Diffusion

1. Introduction

The phenomenon of diffusion may be demonstrated with the use of a diffusion couple, which is formed by joining bars of two different metals together so that there is intimate contact between the two faces.

The types of diffusion:
Interdiffusion (= inpurity diffusion)
Self-diffusion

2. Diffusion Mechanisms

For an atom to make such a move, two conditions must be met:
(1) There must be an empty adjacent site.
(2) The atom must have sufficient energy (vibrational energy) to break bonds with its neighbor atoms and then cause some lattice distortion during the displacement.

Vacancy Diffusion
An atom from a normal lattice position to an adjacent vacant lattice site or vacancy, as represented schematically.

Interstitial Diffusion
This mechanism is found for interdiffusion of impurities such as hydrogen, carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen, which have atoms that are small enough to into the interstitial positions.

3. Fick’s First Law

Diffusion is a time-dependent process- that is, in a macroscopic sense, the quantity of an element that is transported within another is a function of time.
Often it is necessary to know how fast diffusion occurs, or the rate of mass transfer.
The rate is frequently expressed as diffusion flux(J), defined as the mass M diffusing through and perpendicular to a unit cross-sectional area of solid per unit of time.

<1> Quantitative definition

M: The mass
A: The area across which diffusion is occuring
t: The elapsed(경과된) difffusion time

<2> Theoretical model Fick’s First law
Relationship between diffusion flux and concentration gradient!

D: The diffusion coefficient

The constant of proportionality(비례상수) D is called diffusion coefficient, which is expressed in square meters per second. The negative sign in this expression indicates that the direction of diffusion is down the concentration gradient, from a high to a low concentration.

The mass of diffusing species entering the plate on the high-pressure side is equal to the mass exiting from the low-pressure surface (즉, 높은 압력 측에서 판에 들어오는 확산 물질의 질량이 낮은 압력 측에서 나가는 질량과 같음) - such that there is no net accumulation of diffusing species in the plate. This is an example of what is termed steady-state diffusion.
