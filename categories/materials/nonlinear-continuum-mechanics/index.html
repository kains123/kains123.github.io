The Levi-Civita symbol is used to simplify expressions involving cross products in three dimensions.

2.3.5 Tensor analysis

1. Gradient of a Tensor Field
The gradient of a function is the collection of its partial derivatives. When we apply the gradient operator to a tensor field, the order of the resulting tensor increases by one.

$$
[ \text{grad}, u ]{ij} = [ \nabla u ]{ij} = \frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x_j} = u_{i,j}.
$$

1.1 Partial Derivative

$$
\frac{\partial (\cdot)}{\partial xi} = (\cdot){,i}.
$$

2. Divergence Operator

The divergence of a vector or tensor field is defined. The divergence measures the “spread” or “flux” of a vector field.

Divergence of a Vector Field

$$\text{div}, u = \nabla \cdot u = \frac{\partial ui}{\partial x_i} = u{i,i}.$$
