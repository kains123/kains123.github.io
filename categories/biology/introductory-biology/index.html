1. Introduction to Rheology

1. Chemical bonding and molecular interactions

98% H, C, N, O, P, S
Others: Na, Mg, K, Ca, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn
Even smaller amount: Cr, Mo, W, Se, I

There are many types of bonds

  • Covalent bonds
  • Ionic bonds
  • Hydrogen bonds : Occur between polar molecules, such as molecules of water, because of the partial negative charge on the O and the partial postive charge on the H.
  • Hydrophobic interactions : In aqueous environment, the hydrophobic molecules tend to aggregate with one another rather than interact with polar water molecules. (물 분자와 잘 상호작용하지 않는 비극성 분자들이 물 분자와의 접촉을 최소화하기 위해 서로 뭉치는 경향에서 발생)
  • Van Der Waals forces (VDW) : Occur when atoms of two molecules are in close proximity. (분자 전체의 순간적인 쌍극자 모멘트)

1.2 Biological macromolecules

All the cells have lipids, carbonhydrates, nucleic acids and proteins.

1.3 Lipids (지질)

Lipids or fats are predominately hydrocarbon chains that are used as energy storage and insulation. Lipids are hydrophobic in nature. Cell membranes are composed of liquid bilayers, which seperate the aqueous inside of the cell from the aqueous outside of the cell.

1.4 Proteins

Proteins are linear chains of amino acids of which there are 20 distinct types. The order of the amino acids in the chain dictates the shape and the function of the protein in the cell.

Protein Folding (Protein structure) is described by:

  1. Primary Sequence : Based on covalent(Peptides Bonds)
  2. Secondary Structure ; -> Based on Non-Covalent(공유결합 X) interactions (helix sturcture) They are keys of non-covalent forces
  3. Tertiary Structure (접힘 구조); -> Based on Non-Covalent(공유결합 X) interactions
    Ex) Side chain - side chain, peptide “backbone”, water
  4. Quaternary Structure ; -> Based on Non-Covalent(공유결합 X) interactions

The polypeptide chain of protein is always written in an N -> C direction (biosynthesized in an N -> C direction.)
